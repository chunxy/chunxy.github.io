    Composited Exponential Functions If \(\lim_{x \to x_0}f(x) = A, \lim_{x \to x_0}g(x) = B\), then suppose \(\lim_{x \to x_0}f(x)^{g(x)}\) exists, then, \[ \begin{aligned} \ln(\lim_{x \to x_0}f(x)^{g(x)}) &= \lim_{x \to x_0}\ln(f(x)^{g(x)}) \\ &= \lim_{x \to x_0}g(x)\ln(f(x)) \\ &= \lim_{x \to x_0}g(x) \cdot \lim_{x \to x_0}\ln(f(x)) \\ &= B\ln\lim_{x \to x_0}f(x) \\ &= B\ln A \\ \lim_{x \to x_0}f(x)^{g(x)} &= e^{B\ln A} \\ &= (e^{\ln A})^B \\ &= A^B \end{aligned} \] Above deduction is not rigid, but if you want the result only, it is enough.
