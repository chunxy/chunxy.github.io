Entropy

Entropy

The Entropy of discrete distribution \(p\) (probability mass function) is defined as \[ H(p) = -\mathrm{E}_{x \sim p}\log p(x) \] The Entropy of continuous distribution \(q\) (probability density function) is usually similarly defined as \[ H(q) = -\E_{x\sim q}\log q(x) = -\int q(x)\log q(x)dx \] This is actually the Differential Entropy introduced by Shannon. In fact, it is not a good continuous analog of discrete entropy and it was not rigorously derived. For example, this formula can be negative. Therefore, in the case of Entropy, the random variable had better be discrete, although the differential entropy is widely used.
