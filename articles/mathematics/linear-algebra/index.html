Linear Algebra

  • Eigenvectors and Eigenvalues

    Eigenvectors and Eigenvalues Definition An eigenvector of a \(n \times n\) matrix \(A\) is a nonzero vector \(\rm x\) such that \(A\rm x = \lambda \rm x\) for some scalar \(\lambda\).

  • Laplace Expansion

    Laplace Expansion Also known as Cofactor Expansion, Laplace Expansion is an expression of an \(n \times n\) matrix as the weighted sum of determinants of some \((n-1) \times (n-1)\) sub-matrices.

  • Matrix Identity

    Matrix Identity A useful matrix identity \[ (P^{-1}+B^TR^{-1}B)^{-1}B^TR^{-1} = PB^T(BPB^T+R)^{-1} \] can be proved with \[ \begin{aligned} (P^{-1}+B^TR^{-1}B)^{-1}B^TR^{-1} &= PB^T(BPB^T+R)^{-1} \iff\\ B^TR^{-1} &= (P^{-1}+B^TR^{-1}B)PB^T(BPB^T+R)^{-1} \iff\\ B^TR^{-1} &= (P^{-1}PB^T+B^TR^{-1}BPB^T)(BPB^T+R)^{-1} \iff\\ B^TR^{-1} &= (B^T+B^TR^{-1}BPB^T)(BPB^T+R)^{-1} \iff\\ B^TR^{-1} &= (B^TR^{-1}R+B^TR^{-1}BPB^T)(BPB^T+R)^{-1} \iff\\ B^TR^{-1} &= B^TR^{-1}(R+BPB^T)(BPB^T+R)^{-1} \iff\\ B^TR^{-1} &= B^TR^{-1} \\ \end{aligned} \] Its reduced form \[ (I_N+AB)^{-1}A = A(I_M+BA)^{-1} \] can be proved with \[ \begin{aligned} (I_N+AB)^{-1}A &= A(I_M+BA)^{-1} \iff\\ A &= (I_N + AB)A(I_M + BA)^{-1} \iff\\ A &= (A + ABA)(I_M + BA)^{-1} \iff\\ A &= A(I_M + BA)(I_M + BA)^{-1} \iff\\ A &= A \end{aligned} \]

  • Measures

    Chebyshev Distance Chebyshev Distance Discrete form Chebyshev distance is a specific form of Minkowski norm (\(l_p\) norm): \[ \begin{aligned} d_p(x, x^\prime) &= ||x - x^\prime||_p \\ &= (\sum_{i=1}^n|x_i - x^\prime_i|^p)^{1/p} \text{, where $p \to \infty$ } \end{aligned} \] Chebyshev distance is in fact \(\mathop{max}\limits_i (|x_i - x^\prime_i|)\).

  • Real Symmetric Matrix

    Real Symmetric Matrix Assume \(A\) is a \(n \times n\) real-valued symmetric matrix, then its eigenvalues and thus eigenvectors are real-valued Suppose \(A\) has some imaginary eigenvalue \(\lambda\), the corresponding imaginary eigenvector being \(x\): \[ \begin{aligned} Ax &= \lambda x \\ A(x_{real} + x_{img}) &= (\lambda_{real} + \lambda_{img})(x_{real} + x_{img}) \\ Ax_{real} + Ax_{img} &= (\lambda_{real}x_{real} + \lambda_{img}x_{img}) + (\lambda_{real}x_{real} + \lambda_{img}x_{real}) \\ \end{aligned} \] Suppose \(\lambda\)’s and \(x\)’s complex conjugate are \(\bar \lambda\) and \(\bar x\) respectively.
